**Why I am writing to you.** The Chess Algorithm (CA) stands squarely in the research tradition you have shaped. Its development schedule adopts the linearly decaying coefficient you introduced in the Grey Wolf Optimizer; its experimental protocol follows the benchmarking and statistical-testing standards established across your GWO, WOA, SCA, and SSA papers; and GWO itself serves as the strongest baseline in our comparisons — a position it earns on the classical suite, as the manuscript reports candidly. It would be difficult to name a body of work that has influenced this project more directly than yours. There is also a personal thread: I was a provincial chess champion in my school years, and this algorithm is my attempt to bring the strategic vocabulary of that game — development, sacrifice, pinning, castling — into the field your work opened for me.

**What the work contains.** CA is a population-based metaheuristic with a heterogeneous-role architecture: agents act as chess pieces (King, Queens, Rooks, Bishops, Knights, Pawns), each with a movement operator abstracted from the corresponding piece's geometry, while chess strategy supplies the control mechanisms — development, sacrifice, pinning, castling, en passant, and a threefold-repetition diversity rule. On six classical 30-D benchmarks, CA significantly outperforms GA, PSO, and SA on most of the suite, while GWO remains stronger on the classical functions — a result we report without embellishment. The transportation side is where CA earns its keep: on an eight-intersection arterial signal coordination problem it matches the best solution found by any competitor, and in an extended comparison against six algorithms from the independent `mealpy` library (WOA, SCA, ALO, MFO, HHO, DE) on two transportation problems — including a continuous berth allocation instance with a known global optimum — CA's standing is assessed against implementations none of us wrote, which I hope you will agree is the fairest possible test.

**What I would ask of you.** Should you accept, I would be grateful for your critical review of the algorithmic formulation and experimental design, and for any refinements you consider necessary — component ablations, CEC-suite extensions, and scalability studies are natural candidates on which your guidance would be invaluable. I will of course prepare whatever revisions, materials, or analyses you require at every stage, and I remain the corresponding author for all administrative purposes unless you prefer otherwise. Authorship order would remain as proposed (yourself as second author), though I am entirely open to your views on this and on the contribution statement.
