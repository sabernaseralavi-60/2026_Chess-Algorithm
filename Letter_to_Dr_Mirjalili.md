**Why I am writing to you.** The Chess Algorithm (CA) stands squarely in the research tradition you have shaped. Its development schedule adopts the linearly decaying coefficient you introduced in the Grey Wolf Optimizer; its experimental protocol follows the benchmarking and statistical-testing standards established across your GWO, WOA, SCA, and SSA papers; and GWO itself serves as the strongest baseline in our comparisons — a position it earns on the classical suite, as the manuscript reports candidly. It would be difficult to name a body of work that has influenced this project more directly than yours.

**What the work contains.** CA is a population-based metaheuristic with a heterogeneous-role architecture: agents act as chess pieces (King, Queens, Rooks, Bishops, Knights, Pawns), each with a movement operator abstracted from the corresponding piece's geometry, while chess strategy supplies the algorithmic control mechanisms — development (exploration schedule), sacrifice (spread-scaled Metropolis acceptance restricted to minor pieces), pinning (progressive variable fixation), castling (safeguarded elite block-exchange), en passant (self-adaptive local capture with a success rule), and a threefold-repetition diversity rule. On six classical 30-D benchmarks, CA significantly outperforms GA, PSO, and SA on the majority of the suite, while GWO remains stronger on the classical functions — a result we report without embellishment. On an eight-intersection arterial signal coordination problem with a Webster/HCM delay model, CA is statistically indistinguishable from GA, PSO, and GWO and attains the best solution found by any method.
